## Our pledge

Sdl3Sharp.Ffi and its maintainers are committed to making participation in this project a welcoming and dignified experience for everyone. This document is not a list of rules imposed from above. It is a statement of the values this project stands behind and pledges to uphold and protect.

We trust everyone who participates in this project to be a person of good faith. That trust is extended freely and unconditionally from the start, and only reconsidered if clearly and demonstrably proven otherwise.

**"Project maintainers"**, as used throughout this document, refers to the individuals responsible for the ongoing development, governance, and moderation of this project.

## Our values

Human dignity and personal individuality are the foundation of everything stated in this document. They are non-negotiable and shall not be violated in any way or form. All other values expressed here flow from this foundation.

**Freedom of expression.** Every person is free to express themselves however they choose, whether that concerns who they are, how they want to be perceived, or how they communicate with others.

**Acceptance and respect.** Everyone is encouraged to accept others as they are and, where possible, to interact with others in the way those others want to be interacted with. Deliberate or intentional violations of this, such as intentionally misgendering someone, are not acceptable.

**Mutual freedom.** Personal freedom is boundless up to the point where it begins to infringe on the freedom of others. Claiming the freedoms described here comes with the responsibility of not denying those same freedoms to others.

**Tolerance.** This project is as tolerant as it can possibly be, and expects the same from everyone who participates in it. Intolerance, however, is not something this project is willing to tolerate in any amount or form.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces and interactions within this project: issues, pull requests, code reviews, discussions, and any other form of participation in the project's repositories.

Behavior outside of these spaces is not something this project monitors or polices. However, a demonstrated pattern of conduct outside this project that is clearly and severely at odds with the values stated here may be taken into consideration when accepting contributions or granting access to project spaces.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable within this project, regardless of intent:

- Harassment of any kind, including sustained unwanted contact or attention.
- Hate speech or discriminatory language targeting any individual or group.
- Sexual language, imagery, or advances of any kind in any project space.
- Personal attacks, insults, or deliberate humiliation.
- Trolling or deliberately disruptive behavior.
- Intimidation, threats, or any attempt to make another person feel unsafe.
- Publishing or exposing private information about a person without their explicit consent.
- Dishonesty, manipulation, or any form of bad faith conduct.

## Enforcement

Reports of violations of this Code of Conduct can be submitted to the project maintainers at the following address:

**[sdl3sharp-conduct@protonmail.com](mailto:sdl3sharp-conduct@protonmail.com)**

Reports are handled with discretion. Neither the reporter nor the person being reported will be unnecessarily exposed, and no action will be taken on the basis of unsubstantiated presumption alone.

Upon receiving a report, the project maintainers will review it and arrive at a collective decision. Depending on the nature and severity of the violation, the outcome may be a formal warning or an outright ban from participating in the project. A pattern of repeated violations, even individually minor ones, may also result in a ban.

A verdict may be lifted if the person in question publicly and credibly demonstrates genuine remorse and meaningful atonement, and the project maintainers collectively decide that such a lifting is appropriate. Any such lifting will be made publicly transparent, so that those who may have been affected are aware of it.
